VA Renovation Loan — The Option Most Veterans Never Hear About

Buy a fixer-upper with zero down AND finance the repairs · VA + Renovation in one loan
The "I didn't know that was an option" moment: As a VA-eligible veteran, you can buy a home that needs work AND finance the repairs — all in one loan, with zero down payment. Most real estate agents don't even know this program exists.
Standard VA loans won't finance a home that needs significant repairs. That's why so many veterans feel priced out of older neighborhoods or fixer-uppers. The VA renovation loan changes that entirely. You're not limited to move-in-ready homes — and you don't need to use your own cash to fix them up.
How It Works

One Loan. One Closing. Zero Down.

The VA renovation loan combines your purchase loan and your renovation budget into a single product. The lender approves you based on the after-improved value of the home — meaning they lend against what the home will be worth once the work is done, not what it's worth today.

You identify the property, get a contractor estimate, and the lender funds both the purchase and the renovation at closing. Renovation funds are held in escrow and released as work is completed and inspected.

Down Payment
Zero. Same as a standard VA loan. Your entitlement covers the full purchase + renovation amount.
What Can Be Financed
Kitchens, bathrooms, roofing, HVAC, electrical, plumbing, flooring, windows, accessibility modifications, and more.
Loan Limits
No limit for veterans with full entitlement. The project is capped at the after-improved appraised value.
Funding Fee
Same as standard VA — 2.15% first use, waived for any service-connected disability rating.
What It's Good For

Homes That Don't Qualify for a Standard VA Loan

The VA has minimum property requirements (MPRs). A standard VA loan will not go through on a home with a failing roof, broken HVAC, or significant structural issues. Those homes get rejected at appraisal — and most veterans walk away.

With a VA renovation loan, those same homes become opportunities. You buy them below market value, finance the repairs, and move in after the work is done — often with immediate equity built in.

Ideal Properties

Older Windcrest homes needing kitchens or baths. SA fixer-uppers with good bones. Estate sales in Terrell Hills or Alamo Heights. Properties with deferred maintenance that other buyers pass on because they assume they need cash. You don't.

What to Watch Out For

The Fine Print

Not all lenders offer this product. Most banks and many mortgage companies don't do VA renovation loans because they require more coordination than a standard loan. You need a lender who specializes in this — not just one who says they can do it.

You'll need a licensed, insured contractor lined up before underwriting — not after. The scope of work must be finalized, estimated, and approved. This adds 2-4 weeks to the process compared to a standard purchase.

Luxury upgrades and additions (adding square footage) are generally not eligible. The program covers repairs and updates, not major additions.
