The VA Home Buyer's Guide

Plain-English guide for military families buying in San Antonio
I work with a lot of military families here in San Antonio — JBSA, Lackland, Ft. Sam Houston, Randolph, Camp Bullis. The same thing comes up over and over: "We didn't know we could do that." This guide is here to fix that. No sales pitch. Just the full picture of what you've earned and how to use it.

Using Your Benefit More Than Once
↺
You Can Use This Loan Over and Over
Entitlement restores · multiple VA loans open at once · PCS exception

Your VA benefit never expires. Full entitlement restores when your prior VA loan is paid off and the property is sold. You can also have multiple VA loans open at the same time, subject to your county's loan cap.

PCS exception: If you receive orders, you may be able to rent your current home and buy again with VA before the standard one-year mark. Talk to a VA-approved lender — this is one of the most underused exceptions in the program.

Building Wealth with Your VA Loan
🌱
Strategy 1 — The Farm
Buy · live 1 year · rent · repeat

Buy a primary residence with your VA loan. Live there one year or until PCS orders. Rent it out — hire a property manager or list on Zillow. Buy your next home with your VA loan. Repeat. Every house becomes a rental. Every rental builds equity. You keep farming.

🏘
Strategy 2 — House Hacking
2–4 unit property · live in one · rent the rest

VA loans allow purchases up to 4-unit properties — as long as you live in one unit. Up to 75% of rental income counts toward your qualification (6 months cash reserves required). Tenants cover most or all of the mortgage while you build equity. For single service members: roommates cover the payment, you own the asset.

📈
Strategy 3 — Equity to Next Investment
HELOC or cash-out refi after equity builds

After 3–5 years you may have $50–100K in equity. A HELOC gives you a line of credit to use as a down payment on a rental property. A cash-out refi lets you pull equity if rates drop and roll it into your next investment. Use intentionally — a HELOC is a second mortgage and must be repaid.

Selling Your VA Home Later
🔒
The Assumable Loan Advantage
Anyone can assume your rate when you sell

99% of VA loans are assumable. When you sell, any buyer — not just veterans — can take over your loan balance and your locked-in interest rate. If you bought at 3% and the market is at 7%, your rate becomes a major selling advantage. Buyers will pay more for your home just to take over that payment.

Non-veteran assumes
Your entitlement stays tied up until they sell or refi
Veteran assumes
Entitlement transfers · yours is freed immediately

Gap between sales price and loan balance must be covered with cash or secondary financing. Timeline: 2–6 months to process.

Specialty VA Programs
🏗
VA One-Time Close Construction Loan
Buy land + build in one loan · no payments during construction

Finance land and construction together — single closing, no payments while your home is being built. Can build: single family, barndominium, multifamily (up to 4 units), tiny home, manufactured home, ADUs, garages, pools. Builder must be VA-approved. Typically requires 620–640+ credit score. Budget for architectural plans and closing costs upfront.

🔧
VA Renovation Loan
Purchase + repairs in one loan

Finance a home and its repairs in a single loan — great for properties that need work. Repair cap typically $50–75K. Eligible: kitchen, bath, HVAC, roof, flooring, mold remediation, accessibility upgrades. All repairs completed within 120 days by a VA-approved contractor. Home must appraise at purchase price + repair cost.

↓
VA IRRRL — Streamline Refinance
Already in a VA loan · no appraisal · no credit check

When rates drop, the IRRRL is your fast lane. No appraisal, no credit check, no income verification. Roll closing costs into the loan. Must have made 6 on-time payments first. Payment must decrease. Rule of thumb: a 1%+ rate drop is worth it. Can be done unlimited times.

Common VA Loan Myths — Busted

"I can only use my VA loan once."
False. It's a lifetime benefit. Use it over and over as long as entitlement is available.
"Sellers won't accept VA offers."
In today's San Antonio market, a well-structured VA offer is competitive. The right agent knows how to write it.
"I need perfect credit."
The VA sets no minimum. Most lenders require 580–620+. Many veterans qualify with less-than-perfect credit.
"I can't buy a fixer-upper."
That's what the VA Renovation Loan is for.
"I have to live in the home forever."
Live there one year, then rent it out and buy again. That's the farm strategy.
"A VA appraisal will kill the deal."
VA appraisals check safety and habitability — minimum property requirements. For a standard move-in ready home, this rarely causes issues.
