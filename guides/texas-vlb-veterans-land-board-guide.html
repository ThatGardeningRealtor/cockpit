Texas Veterans Land Board — Three Programs Most Texas Veterans Never Use

Home loans · Land loans · Home improvement loans · All at below-market rates · Texas veterans only
The "I didn't know that was an option" moment: If you served in the US military and live in Texas, the state has three separate loan programs reserved specifically for you — all at below-market interest rates. Most Texas veterans never use them because nobody told them they exist. The VA loan is federal. The Texas VLB programs are on top of that.
The Texas Veterans Land Board (VLB) is a state agency managed by the Texas General Land Office. It has been lending to Texas veterans since 1946. These programs are separate from federal VA benefits and can be used alongside them. A Texas veteran can potentially use a federal VA loan AND a VLB loan simultaneously for different properties.
The Three Programs

What the VLB Actually Offers

🏠
VLB Home Loan
Below-market interest rate. Up to $726,200. 30-year term. Works statewide.
🏞️
VLB Land Loan
5+ acres minimum. Below-market rate. 30-year term. Only land loan program at this rate in Texas.
🔨
Home Improvement Loan
Up to $50,000. Below-market rate. Fix, update, or improve your current home.
The stack nobody talks about: A Texas veteran can combine the VLB Land Loan + VLB Home Improvement Loan to buy rural land and then improve it — two separate VLB loans running simultaneously.
Who Qualifies

Eligibility Requirements

You must be a Texas resident who served in the US Armed Forces — Army, Navy, Air Force, Marines, Coast Guard, Space Force, National Guard, or Reserve. Active duty members also qualify. There is no minimum service requirement for active duty. For veterans, you generally need an honorable or general discharge.

Surviving spouses of eligible veterans may also qualify. The VLB website has a full eligibility check tool at vlb.texas.gov.

The Land Loan

Why This Matters for Land Buyers in South Texas

Conventional lenders won't finance raw rural land at reasonable rates. Capital Farm Credit and Texas Farm Credit typically require 15-20% down on 15-year terms. The VLB land loan offers a 30-year term at a below-market rate — and only requires 5% down.

For Texas veterans looking at recreational land, hunting properties, or rural acreage in Medina, Atascosa, McMullen, or surrounding counties, the VLB land loan is often the best financing available — and almost nobody uses it.

Minimum: 5 acres. The property must be in Texas. There is no maximum acreage limit.

How to Use It

Getting Started

Apply directly through the VLB at vlb.texas.gov or through a VLB-approved lender. The process is similar to a conventional mortgage — credit check, income verification, appraisal. The VLB does not require a VA Certificate of Eligibility because it is a state program with its own eligibility determination.

Processing typically takes 30-45 days, similar to a standard mortgage. The VLB works with a network of approved lenders who handle the origination.
