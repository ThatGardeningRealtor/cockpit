The Short Version

USDA loans let eligible buyers purchase a home with zero down payment, lower rates than conventional, and fixed monthly payments — in eligible rural and suburban areas surrounding San Antonio. Many SA suburbs qualify.

Two Types of USDA Loans

1. USDA Guaranteed Loan

Offered through private lenders. USDA guarantees the loan — lender takes less risk and passes savings to you. This is the most common type most buyers will use. Income limits apply.

2. USDA Direct Loan

Funded directly by the USDA — not a private lender. For very low-income individuals and families. Even more flexible terms. Current rate: 5.00% fixed.

Do You Qualify?

Location

Property must be in a USDA-eligible area. Many SA suburbs qualify including: Schertz, Cibolo, Converse, Selma, Universal City, Marion, Floresville, Pleasanton, Castroville, Lytle, Natalia, Poteet, Devine and more. Check the USDA eligibility map at usda.gov.

Income

Income limits vary by county and household size. Guaranteed loan = moderate income. Direct loan = very low income. Check current limits at usda.gov.

Credit

No hard minimum — lenders typically want 640+. Manual underwriting available for lower scores.

How to Get Started
  1. Check your location at the USDA eligibility map (usda.gov)
  2. Verify your income qualifies for your area and household size
  3. Get pre-approved through a USDA-approved lender
  4. Find eligible properties in qualifying areas
  5. Work with your agent to submit an offer

Great for: Buyers in SA suburbs with limited savings, first-time buyers, buyers looking outside the Loop 410 / 1604 corridors, anyone who wants space to grow a garden or live rurally without giving up proximity to SA.
