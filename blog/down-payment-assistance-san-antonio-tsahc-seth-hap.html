Down Payment Assistance in San Antonio 2026: TSAHC, SETH GoldStar, and City HAP Explained

By Chris Drawdy · June 5, 2026 · 10 min read

If you've been putting off buying a home in San Antonio because you can't quite pull together a down payment, 2026 might be the year everything changes for you. Between state-level programs like TSAHC and SETH GoldStar, plus San Antonio's own Homeownership Assistance Program, there's more free money on the table right now than most buyers ever realize. This guide breaks down every major program available, how to qualify, and — crucially — how to layer them together so you're walking into your new home with as little out of pocket as possible.

Why San Antonio Is One of the Best Cities in Texas to Buy With DPA

San Antonio sits in a genuinely rare position: it has access to state programs, a regional housing fund, AND a city-level assistance program that many Texas metros simply don't offer. That overlap creates real stacking opportunities for buyers who know what they're doing. Think of it like companion planting — the right combinations produce something far more powerful than any single program could on its own.

The city's median home price as of early 2026 is hovering around $285,000, which keeps most properties well within program purchase price limits. Neighborhoods like Converse, Kirby, Lackland City, and the South Side along Palo Alto Road are particularly well-suited for DPA buyers because values remain accessible while still qualifying under program caps.

San Antonio is also home to a dense network of HUD-approved housing counselors, approved lenders, and nonprofit partners who work these programs daily. That infrastructure matters — it means you won't be navigating this alone, and turnaround times on approvals tend to be faster here than in smaller Texas markets.

TSAHC: Texas State Affordable Housing Corporation Programs

TSAHC is the flagship statewide program and the one most San Antonio buyers encounter first. It operates two main tracks: the Homes for Texas Heroes program (for teachers, first responders, veterans, nurses, and corrections officers) and the Home Sweet Texas program (for all other income-qualifying buyers). Both offer the same core benefit — a grant of 3% to 5% of your loan amount that never has to be repaid.

On a $280,000 FHA loan, a 5% TSAHC grant equals $14,000 handed directly to your closing. That covers your entire 3.5% FHA down payment with room to spare toward closing costs. For Heroes program participants — think NEISD or Northside ISD teachers, SAPD officers, or Brooke Army Medical Center nurses — there's also a slightly reduced mortgage rate that compounds the savings over time.

TSAHC Eligibility at a Glance

Income limits for Bexar County in 2026 fall around $97,000–$110,000 depending on household size and whether you're using the Heroes or Home Sweet Texas track. The purchase price limit is approximately $481,000 for most loan types, which covers the overwhelming majority of homes in San Antonio's current market. You'll need a minimum 620 credit score for FHA and 640 for conventional.

One important note: TSAHC does NOT require you to be a first-time homebuyer on the Heroes program. If you're a veteran or a teacher who owned a home five years ago and sold it, you can still qualify. That surprises a lot of people and opens the door for more experienced buyers who assumed they were locked out.

SETH GoldStar: The Regional Program That Often Beats TSAHC

Southeast Texas Housing Finance Corporation — SETH — runs the GoldStar program, and it's genuinely underused in San Antonio. GoldStar offers assistance of 3% to 7% of the loan amount, structured as a soft second lien rather than a grant. In most cases, that lien is forgiven after three years of continued homeownership, which means it effectively functions like a grant for buyers who stay put.

The 7% tier is the headline number, but it comes with a slightly higher interest rate on your first mortgage. The sweet spot for most San Antonio buyers tends to be the 5% assistance tier, which keeps your rate competitive while still covering the full FHA down payment. Your SETH-approved lender will run the numbers on both options so you can compare total monthly cost.

Where SETH GoldStar Stands Apart

SETH has slightly more flexible income limits than TSAHC in some household configurations, and it's available for both FHA and conventional loans. For buyers eyeing homes along the 1604 loop in areas like Leon Valley, Helotes, or Universal City — slightly higher price points — SETH's higher purchase price ceiling can be the deciding factor. The program is also open to non-first-time buyers across the board, not just Heroes.

One practical detail: SETH requires homebuyer education from an approved counselor before closing. In San Antonio, Catholic Charities of San Antonio and the San Antonio Housing Authority both offer HUD-approved counseling — often for free or at very low cost — and they're well-versed in GoldStar requirements specifically.

San Antonio's City HAP: The Local Program Most Buyers Miss

The City of San Antonio Homeownership Assistance Program — HAP — is administered through the city's Department of Human Services and provides up to $30,000 in down payment and closing cost assistance for income-qualifying buyers purchasing within San Antonio city limits. This is deferred forgivable loan money, meaning it carries 0% interest and is forgiven after a set compliance period (typically five years for lower amounts, longer for larger awards).

HAP is income-based and targets buyers at or below 80% of Area Median Income (AMI). For a household of four in Bexar County in 2026, that AMI threshold sits around $68,000–$72,000. It's a more targeted program than TSAHC or SETH, but for buyers who qualify, $30,000 is transformative — it can cover closing costs AND the down payment entirely on a modestly priced home.

Where HAP Works Best in San Antonio

HAP is particularly powerful on the West Side (neighborhoods around Edgewood ISD and South San ISD), the East Side along Martin Luther King Drive, and portions of the South Side where you can still find solid homes in the $190,000–$240,000 range. At those price points, HAP assistance can mean buying with essentially nothing out of pocket. It's a genuine path to homeownership for families who've been renting for years and assumed buying was still out of reach.

The HAP application process is more involved than TSAHC or SETH — it's city-administered, funding is allocated in cycles, and waitlists are real. That's why timing matters. I always advise HAP-eligible clients to start the process 60–90 days before they want to be under contract, not the week they fall in love with a house.

How to Stack DPA Programs — and When It's Possible

Here's where it gets exciting. In some scenarios, San Antonio buyers can layer multiple programs on top of each other. The most common and powerful combination is pairing TSAHC or SETH assistance with City HAP funds. TSAHC and SETH cover your down payment; HAP covers your closing costs. Done right, a qualifying buyer can close on a San Antonio home with less than $1,000 out of pocket.

There are rules, of course. Program stacking requires lender approval, and not every lender is approved for every program combination. You'll want a lender who is actively certified for BOTH your chosen state program and HAP — lenders like Cornerstone Home Lending, Legacy Mutual Mortgage, and Benchmark Mortgage have staff in San Antonio who work these combinations regularly. Your lender choice is not a minor detail here; it can make or break a stacked deal.

A Real Stacking Example

Let's say a family of three in the Edgewood ISD area earns $62,000 annually. They find a home on the West Side listed at $215,000. Using TSAHC Home Sweet Texas at 5%, they receive a $10,750 grant covering the FHA down payment ($7,525) with $3,225 left for costs. City HAP contributes up to $20,000 toward remaining closing costs and lender fees. Their actual cash needed at closing might be $500–$1,500 — mostly for inspections and appraisal paid before closing.

That's not a hypothetical. That's a scenario I've helped clients navigate. The key is starting the process early, having your documents in order, and working with a team who knows these programs inside and out. Seeds need time to germinate — so does a DPA deal done right.

What You Need to Prepare Before Applying

Every program in this guide requires documentation, and getting it together early saves weeks of frustration. You'll need two years of tax returns (W-2s and 1040s), 30 days of pay stubs, two months of bank statements, a government-issued ID, and your Social Security number for a credit pull. If you're self-employed, add a year-to-date profit and loss statement.

Homebuyer education is required for all three programs — TSAHC, SETH, and HAP. You only need to take one approved course to satisfy all of them. The online course through eHome America (approved by all programs and about $99) is the most efficient option. SAHA's in-person workshops are free and include one-on-one counseling, which some buyers find more valuable than the certificate itself.

Credit matters too, and not just your score. Lenders will look at your debt-to-income ratio, which must typically stay below 45% on FHA loans. If you're carrying a car note, student loans, or credit card balances, a lender pre-consultation — ideally 3–6 months before you plan to buy — gives you time to prune those numbers into shape before you apply.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use TSAHC or SETH GoldStar if I've owned a home before in San Antonio?

Yes — and this surprises more buyers than almost anything else about these programs. TSAHC's Heroes track (for teachers, first responders, veterans, and healthcare workers) has no first-time buyer requirement whatsoever. SETH GoldStar is also open to repeat buyers across all assistance tiers. The Home Sweet Texas track under TSAHC does require first-time buyer status in non-targeted areas of Bexar County, but certain zip codes along the South Side and East Side are designated as "targeted areas" where the first-time requirement is waived. Your lender can check your specific property address against the targeted area map before you even submit an application.

Does the San Antonio City HAP program have a waitlist, and how long does it take?

HAP funding is released in cycles through the City's Department of Human Services, and yes, there are periods where funds are fully committed or on hold between cycles. As of mid-2026, HAP has been more consistently funded than in some prior years due to federal HOME Investment Partnerships Program allocations, but availability is never guaranteed. The typical timeline from application to approval runs 45–75 days, which is why starting early is critical — you do not want to be under contract on a home and discover HAP funds are exhausted for the cycle. Buyers with urgency sometimes pair HAP with TSAHC or SETH as the primary mechanism and use HAP as a supplemental layer only after confirming funds are available for their projected closing month.

What happens to my SETH GoldStar second lien if I sell my home before three years?

If you sell, refinance, or transfer the property before the forgiveness period expires, the outstanding balance of the SETH second lien becomes due and payable at that transaction. The balance does not accrue interest, but it does need to be settled from your sale proceeds. The good news is that in San Antonio's market, most homes purchased with SETH assistance have appreciated enough by year two or three that the lien payoff is easily handled from equity. If you're considering a move within three years — job transfer, family change, deployment — it's worth calculating that payoff into your exit scenario before choosing the 7% tier, since a larger lien balance means a larger payoff obligation if you sell early.

Are there homes in San Antonio that don't qualify for DPA programs?

Yes, and it's an important point buyers sometimes learn too late. Most DPA programs require the home to be your primary residence — investment properties and second homes are excluded entirely. Manufactured homes and some condo developments that aren't FHA-approved also fall outside program guidelines. Homes priced above the program purchase price ceiling (roughly $481,000 for TSAHC and SETH as of 2026) aren't eligible either. Additionally, HAP requires the property to be within San Antonio city limits, so homes in Converse, Live Oak, or Schertz — even with SA-area addresses — don't qualify for HAP specifically. Always confirm eligibility on the actual property address before going under contract.

What credit score do I realistically need to get approved for DPA programs in San Antonio?

The published minimums are 620 for FHA-based TSAHC and SETH assistance, and 640 for conventional loan tracks within those same programs. HAP itself doesn't set a credit minimum but requires an approved first mortgage, so your lender's score requirement applies indirectly. In practice, buyers with scores between 620–639 sometimes face tighter debt-to-income scrutiny and fewer lender options, since not all DPA-approved lenders work all the way down to 620. A score of 660 or above significantly broadens your lender choices and can unlock better interest rates, which matters because DPA assistance at closing doesn't lower your monthly payment — only your rate does. If you're at 600–615, a 60–90 day credit improvement plan focusing on utilization ratio and disputing errors is often enough to cross the threshold without delaying your purchase significantly.
