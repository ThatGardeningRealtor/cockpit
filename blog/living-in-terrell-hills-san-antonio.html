Living in Terrell Hills, San Antonio: What Buyers Need to Know

By Chris Drawdy · June 5, 2026 · 10 min read

Some neighborhoods in San Antonio feel like they've always been exactly what they are — settled, graceful, and deeply rooted. Terrell Hills is one of those places. Tucked just northeast of downtown and bordered by Alamo Heights and Fort Sam Houston, this small, independent city of roughly 5,000 residents carries the kind of quiet prestige that doesn't need to announce itself. The oak trees do the talking. If you've been curious about Terrell Hills real estate — whether you're relocating to San Antonio, upsizing, or simply looking for a neighborhood that holds its value the way a well-tended garden holds its soil — this guide is for you. Let's dig in.

What Makes Terrell Hills Different From Other San Antonio Neighborhoods?

Terrell Hills is technically its own incorporated city, not a subdivision of San Antonio proper. That distinction matters more than most buyers realize. It means the community has its own city council, its own public works, and a level of local governance that keeps development controlled and the neighborhood character intact. You won't find a strip mall suddenly appearing on a corner lot here. That kind of intentional stewardship is rare in a fast-growing metro like San Antonio, and it's a big reason why Terrell Hills homes hold their value so reliably over time.

The neighborhood was developed primarily in the 1930s through 1950s, and that legacy shows in the architecture. Tudor Revivals, Spanish Eclectic cottages, Georgian Colonials, and mid-century ranches line streets shaded by mature live oaks and pecan trees. Many of these lots are generous — often quarter-acre or larger — giving residents room for the kind of outdoor living that San Antonio's long growing season practically demands.

A Community That Takes Pride in Its Appearance

Drive through Terrell Hills on any Saturday morning and you'll see residents out tending their yards, walking dogs, and waving to neighbors. There's a genuine civic pride here. The Terrell Hills Garden Club has been active in the community for decades, and you'll notice it in the landscaping — established beds of Texas sage, esperanza, and roses that have had years to mature into something truly beautiful. As someone who believes a well-loved yard is one of the best indicators of a well-loved neighborhood, I find that deeply reassuring.

Location, Location, Location — Terrell Hills Nails It

If you drew a circle around the most desirable destinations in San Antonio and looked for the sweet spot at the center, you'd land somewhere close to Terrell Hills. The neighborhood sits along the Broadway corridor, one of the city's most vibrant arteries, connecting residents to virtually everything with minimal effort.

What's Nearby

  • The Pearl District — About 10 minutes southwest, San Antonio's beloved mixed-use destination with farmers markets, restaurants, and Hotel Emma is practically a backyard amenity.
  • Brackenridge Park — The city's oldest park, with the San Antonio Zoo, Japanese Tea Garden, and miles of trails, borders the neighborhood to the west.
  • Fort Sam Houston — The base is essentially adjacent, making Terrell Hills a top choice for military families and Department of Defense employees seeking off-base housing.
  • The San Antonio Medical Center — A roughly 15-minute drive, with UT Health San Antonio, University Hospital, and dozens of specialty practices easily accessible.
  • Downtown San Antonio — The River Walk, the Alamo, and the convention center are about 15 minutes away without highway stress.
  • Broadway restaurant row — Restaurants like Cured, Kimura, and Carriqui are just minutes from most Terrell Hills addresses.

For buyers who want walkability layered over a true neighborhood feel, Terrell Hills delivers. You can walk to coffee, walk to the park, and still have the privacy of a home with a real backyard.

Terrell Hills Real Estate: What the Market Looks Like

Let me be straightforward: Terrell Hills San Antonio homes are not entry-level, and they were never meant to be. This is one of the city's most established prestige markets, and pricing reflects that. As of mid-2026, the median home price in Terrell Hills typically falls in the $650,000 to $1.2 million range, with exceptional properties — larger lots, updated interiors, pool — routinely closing above $1.5 million.

What Your Budget Gets You

  • $650K–$850K: You're likely looking at a 2,000–2,800 sq ft original-era home that may need updating. Good bones, established lot, classic architecture. Think of it like acquiring a mature garden that needs some intentional replanting.
  • $850K–$1.2M: Move-in ready or recently renovated homes with updated kitchens, modern baths, and often a pool or outdoor living space. This is the sweet spot for buyers who want character without a gut renovation.
  • $1.2M+: Fully reimagined estate homes, larger footprints (3,500+ sq ft), resort-style outdoor spaces, and premium finishes. These properties often attract corporate relocations and buyers coming from higher-cost markets like Austin, Dallas, or out of state.

Market Dynamics

Inventory in Terrell Hills is always limited — the neighborhood simply doesn't turn over quickly. When a well-priced home hits the market, it often receives multiple offers within days. If you're serious about buying here, you need a pre-approval in hand before you start touring, and you need a realtor who knows how to craft a competitive offer in a low-inventory environment. Days on market for desirable properties often run under 20 days. Properties that linger typically have pricing, condition, or disclosure issues worth scrutinizing carefully.

Schools Serving Terrell Hills Residents

One of the most common questions I get from families considering this neighborhood is about schools. The answer requires a little nuance, because Terrell Hills sits within the Alamo Heights Independent School District (AHISD) — and that's a genuinely significant asset.

Alamo Heights ISD Overview

AHISD is consistently one of the highest-rated school districts in all of Texas. The district serves a relatively small geographic footprint, which contributes to its close-knit culture and focused resources. Students in Terrell Hills are zoned to:

  • Woodridge Elementary — Well-regarded, community-oriented, and walkable from much of the neighborhood.
  • Alamo Heights Middle School — Strong academics and a broad range of extracurriculars.
  • Alamo Heights High School — Routinely recognized as one of Texas's top public high schools, with strong AP and dual enrollment programs, competitive athletics, and a college placement track that draws serious families.

For families with children, zoning into AHISD is one of the primary financial and lifestyle reasons to buy in Terrell Hills specifically, versus nearby neighborhoods just outside the district boundary.

Homes, Architecture, and What Buyers Should Expect

If you love new construction with open floor plans and nine-foot ceilings throughout, Terrell Hills may require some adjustment in expectations — or an open mind. The homes here were built in an era when rooms had defined purposes, hallways existed, and dining rooms were formal. Some buyers fall immediately in love with that. Others need a moment to appreciate it.

Architectural Highlights

The dominant styles you'll encounter in the Terrell Hills neighborhood include:

  • Tudor Revival — Steeply pitched rooflines, half-timbering details, arched doorways. These homes photograph beautifully and age remarkably well.
  • Spanish Eclectic / Mediterranean — Red tile roofs, stucco exteriors, interior courtyards. Perfectly suited to San Antonio's climate and aesthetic heritage.
  • Georgian Colonial — Symmetrical brick facades, formal proportions, large windows. Classic and enduring.
  • Mid-Century Ranch — Lower-profile, horizontal lines, often with original terrazzo floors and open carports. These have had a significant resurgence in buyer demand and renovation investment.

What to Watch For in Older Homes

Buying a home built between 1935 and 1965 in San Antonio's climate means you should budget for and expect: foundation inspection (expansive clay soils here move with rainfall cycles), electrical panel updates if the home has original wiring, HVAC system age, and potential presence of lead paint or asbestos in original materials. None of this should scare you off — it's simply the nature of acquiring something with genuine history. A thorough inspection by a licensed Texas inspector, and ideally a structural engineer's review of the foundation, is non-negotiable. Think of it as learning everything about a plant before you decide how to care for it.

Financing a Terrell Hills Home: What Buyers Need to Know

At the price points typical in Terrell Hills, most transactions involve conventional jumbo financing or substantial cash positions. A few practical notes for buyers:

Jumbo Loan Considerations

For 2026, the conforming loan limit for a single-family home in Bexar County sits at $806,500. Anything above that requires a jumbo mortgage, which carries slightly different qualification standards — typically requiring stronger credit scores (720+), lower debt-to-income ratios, and larger reserve requirements. San Antonio-based lenders with strong jumbo programs worth exploring include Frost Bank, which has deep roots in the local market and competitive portfolio products, and Randolph-Brooks Federal Credit Union (RBFCU), particularly popular with military-affiliated buyers given Fort Sam's proximity. National lenders like PrimeLending and Movement Mortgage also have strong San Antonio offices and experienced loan officers who know the luxury segment.

Property Taxes in Terrell Hills

Texas has no state income tax, but property taxes are the trade-off, and buyers need to understand this going in. In Bexar County, effective property tax rates typically run between 1.9% and 2.3% of assessed value annually. On a $900,000 home, that's roughly $17,100–$20,700 per year — a meaningful monthly budget line. Texas's homestead exemption reduces your taxable value by $100,000 for school taxes once you establish the property as your primary residence, and there's an additional exemption for homeowners 65 and older that can significantly cap increases. File your homestead exemption application with the Bexar Appraisal District as soon as you close and establish residency.

Is Terrell Hills Right for You? The Honest Assessment

Terrell Hills isn't for every buyer, and I'd rather help you find the right fit than sell you on something that doesn't match your life. This neighborhood tends to be an exceptional fit for:

  • Families prioritizing Alamo Heights ISD who want to invest in a home rather than rent near the district
  • Professionals relocating from higher-cost metros who want maximum lifestyle per dollar
  • Military families (particularly senior officers or DOD civilians) who want quality off-base housing close to Fort Sam
  • Buyers who appreciate architecture, mature landscaping, and neighborhood permanence over flash
  • Downsizers coming from larger suburban homes who want to trade square footage for location and community

If you want a brand-new home with a builder warranty, a large suburban lot, or a price point under $500,000, Terrell Hills will likely frustrate you. There are wonderful San Antonio neighborhoods — Helotes, Stone Oak, Cibolo — that serve those needs beautifully. But if the idea of a neighborhood that's been carefully tended for nearly a century, where your neighbors have been there for decades and your oak tree was planted before your parents were born, resonates with you — you may have just found your place.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the average home price in Terrell Hills, San Antonio in 2026?

As of mid-2026, the median home price in Terrell Hills generally ranges from approximately $650,000 to $1.2 million, depending on size, condition, and how recently the home has been updated. Fully renovated or larger estate properties regularly close above $1.5 million. Because inventory is limited and demand from quality buyers is consistent, prices in Terrell Hills have historically appreciated at or above the broader San Antonio market average. Buyers should expect to compete for well-priced, move-in-ready homes, particularly in the $800K–$1.1M range, which sees the highest activity. Working with a local realtor who monitors Terrell Hills specifically — rather than relying on broad metro-level data — is the best way to get accurate, current pricing intelligence before you make a move.

What school district serves Terrell Hills, TX?

Terrell Hills is served by the Alamo Heights Independent School District (AHISD), which is consistently ranked among the top school districts in Texas. Students are typically zoned to Woodridge Elementary, Alamo Heights Middle School, and Alamo Heights High School. AHISD is known for strong academic performance, competitive extracurricular programs, and a tight-knit community feel that reflects the neighborhoods it serves. For families, the AHISD zoning is one of the primary reasons to prioritize Terrell Hills specifically over neighboring areas. It's worth confirming your exact zoning during the home search process, as district boundaries can vary slightly by address — your realtor or the AHISD district office can verify this quickly.

Is Terrell Hills a safe neighborhood?

Terrell Hills consistently ranks as one of the safest communities in the greater San Antonio area. As an independently incorporated city, it maintains its own governance structure and benefits from both its own public services and proximity to Bexar County resources. The neighborhood's close-knit culture, high homeownership rates, and engaged community all contribute to a strong sense of safety and accountability. Residents frequently cite the walkability and ease of knowing their neighbors as quality-of-life features they value highly. As with any real estate decision, doing your own due diligence — including reviewing publicly available crime statistics from the City of San Antonio and Bexar County — is always advisable, but Terrell Hills has a longstanding reputation as one of the most secure and stable neighborhoods in the region.

Are there any historic preservation restrictions on Terrell Hills homes?

Many homes in Terrell Hills date from the 1930s through 1950s, and while the neighborhood has a deep architectural heritage, not all properties carry formal historic designation or overlay restrictions at the city or state level. However, Terrell Hills as an incorporated city does maintain its own ordinances governing things like setbacks, lot coverage, and structural changes — and these can affect renovation plans. If you are purchasing a home with the intent to significantly remodel, add square footage, or alter the exterior appearance, you should consult with the Terrell Hills city offices early in your process to understand what permits and approvals are required. Some buyers also choose to work with architects and contractors who specialize in period-appropriate renovation to preserve the home's character and market value. Your realtor can help connect you with professionals experienced in this specific type of work in the San Antonio market.

How competitive is the Terrell Hills real estate market, and how should buyers prepare?

The Terrell Hills real estate market is consistently competitive due to low inventory and steady demand. Well-priced, move-in-ready homes often receive multiple offers within the first week on market, and days on market for desirable properties can be under two weeks. To compete effectively, buyers should have a pre-approval letter from a reputable lender — ideally one experienced with jumbo financing if your purchase price exceeds $806,500 — before touring homes. Cash buyers have a notable advantage in this market, and sellers in this price range often scrutinize the strength of financing carefully. It's also important to have a clear understanding of your non-negotiables versus preferences so you can move confidently when the right home appears. Working with a realtor who actively tracks Terrell Hills inventory and has relationships in the community can make a meaningful difference in getting early access to listings and crafting an offer that resonates with sellers in this distinctive neighborhood.
