Living in Windcrest, TX (78239): A Complete Neighborhood Guide for Home Buyers

By Chris Drawdy · June 5, 2026 · 10 min read

Some neighborhoods are like a well-tended garden — they've been around long enough to develop real character, deep roots, and a community that genuinely looks out for one another. Windcrest, Texas is exactly that kind of place. Tucked into the northeast corner of Bexar County, this small incorporated city sits entirely within the greater San Antonio metro, yet it operates with a distinct small-town identity that home buyers discover with a kind of pleasant surprise. If you've been searching for homes for sale in Windcrest, Texas or trying to understand whether the 78239 neighborhood is the right fit for your family, this guide is written for you — no fluff, no pressure, just honest local knowledge.

What Makes Windcrest, TX Unique?

Windcrest is one of those rare places in the San Antonio area that has managed to stay itself. With a population hovering around 5,500 residents, it's an incorporated municipality — meaning it has its own city government, its own police department, and its own code of ordinances — while sitting completely surrounded by the City of San Antonio. Think of it like a garden bed with its own clearly defined border: everything inside is intentional and cared for in its own particular way.

Founded in the early 1950s as one of San Antonio's first planned communities, Windcrest was developed with an emphasis on green space, winding streets, and single-family residential living. That original vision hasn't been abandoned. Today, the city maintains an unusually high ratio of parkland to residents, and the community's famous Christmas lights display — a tradition going back to the 1960s — draws tens of thousands of visitors from across the metro each December. It's the kind of neighborhood where people choose to stay for decades.

A City Within a City

Because Windcrest is its own municipality, residents enjoy a level of local governance responsiveness that larger neighborhoods simply can't match. City council meetings are accessible, neighbors know their elected officials, and community input genuinely shapes decisions about parks, infrastructure, and development. For buyers coming from sprawling suburban subdivisions, this tends to feel refreshingly human-scale.

Windcrest TX Real Estate: What the Market Looks Like

The Windcrest TX real estate market sits in an interesting position: it offers established, well-maintained homes at price points that remain more attainable than many comparable areas inside Loop 410, while still delivering the stability and appreciation that come with a mature, in-demand community.

As of mid-2026, the median home price in Windcrest generally ranges between $225,000 and $310,000, depending on the size, condition, and specific street. You'll find a mix of brick ranch-style homes from the 1950s through 1980s — many of them larger than they appear from the street, with spacious yards that a gardener like me genuinely appreciates — alongside some more recently updated properties that have been thoughtfully renovated. Unlike many San Antonio neighborhoods where new construction has changed the texture of the streetscape, Windcrest feels cohesive. Most homes sit on lots ranging from 7,000 to over 10,000 square feet, and mature trees are the rule rather than the exception.

What Your Budget Gets You in 78239

  • $200,000–$250,000: Solid 3-bedroom, 1–2 bath brick homes, typically 1,200–1,500 sq ft, often with original character and good bones. Expect to budget for cosmetic updates.
  • $250,000–$300,000: Updated 3–4 bedroom homes with modern kitchens, refreshed bathrooms, and move-in-ready condition. Often feature larger lots with established landscaping.
  • $300,000+: Larger footprints, significant renovations, or premium lot positions. This segment moves quickly when priced correctly.

Property taxes in Windcrest fall under Bexar County, with the city levy added on top. Total effective tax rates in the 78239 zip code typically land in the 2.1%–2.4% range, which is comparable to much of the northeast San Antonio market. Homestead exemptions — including the standard Bexar County exemption, the over-65 freeze, and any applicable disabled veteran exemptions — can meaningfully reduce that burden. If you're a veteran, Texas's property tax exemptions are genuinely significant and worth understanding before you buy anywhere in Bexar County.

Financing Options for Windcrest Buyers

Because most Windcrest homes are well within conventional loan limits, buyers have access to the full range of financing options. VA loans are extremely common in this part of San Antonio given the proximity to Fort Sam Houston, Randolph AFB, and Lackland — many Windcrest families have military ties. FHA loans work well for first-time buyers with lower down payments, and the Texas State Affordable Housing Corporation (TSAHC) offers down payment assistance programs that San Antonio-area buyers frequently use. The My First Texas Home program is another resource worth exploring if you're a first-time buyer or haven't owned a home in the past three years. Local lenders like Generations Federal Credit Union, Security Service Federal Credit Union, and USAA (for military families) all have strong presences in this market and understand the nuances of buying in Bexar County.

Schools Serving the Windcrest Area

Windcrest falls within the Judson Independent School District (JISD), one of the larger school districts serving the northeast San Antonio corridor. For families evaluating the 78239 neighborhood, understanding the school assignment picture is important — and worth a direct conversation with the district, since attendance boundaries can shift.

Schools Commonly Serving Windcrest Residents

  • Windcrest Elementary School — Located directly within the community, this school has a strong sense of neighborhood identity and is a point of pride for many long-term residents.
  • Judson Middle School — Serves many Windcrest students at the middle school level, with programs in fine arts, athletics, and academic enrichment.
  • Wagner High School — The primary high school for Windcrest-area students, offering a range of CTE (Career and Technical Education) pathways, UIL athletics, and a diverse student body reflective of northeast Bexar County.

Families with specific academic priorities — gifted programs, STEM focus, or fine arts emphasis — should also explore Judson ISD's magnet and choice program options, which allow students to apply for specialized tracks across the district. The district's transportation network is reasonably well-organized for the northeast corridor.

Location and Commute: Where Windcrest Sits in the Metro

One of the most practical arguments for Windcrest is its location. Positioned near the intersection of IH-35 and Loop 410 on the northeast side of San Antonio, residents enjoy genuinely convenient access to major employment centers across the metro.

Key Distances and Drive Times from Windcrest

  • Fort Sam Houston / JBSA-FSH: Approximately 10–15 minutes — one reason military families frequently settle here.
  • Randolph Air Force Base: 15–20 minutes via IH-35 North through Universal City.
  • Downtown San Antonio: 20–25 minutes depending on traffic on IH-35 or Loop 410.
  • San Antonio International Airport: 15–20 minutes.
  • The Medical Center / South Texas Medical Center: 30–40 minutes via Loop 410 West.
  • Northeast Methodist Hospital / Christus Santa Rosa: Both within 15–20 minutes.

VIA Metropolitan Transit serves the broader 78239 area, though like most of the northeast side, the neighborhood is designed around car ownership. Having a vehicle is essentially assumed in everyday life here.

Community Life and Lifestyle in Windcrest

This is where Windcrest really earns its reputation. The community has a genuine civic culture that's rare to find in a suburb of San Antonio's size. The Parks and Recreation Department maintains several parks, a recreation center, and programming throughout the year. Windcrest City Park is a lovely gathering place — the kind of green, shaded space that reminds you why established neighborhoods with mature trees are worth seeking out.

The Famous Windcrest Christmas Lights

Every December, Windcrest transforms into what is widely considered one of the most spectacular residential holiday light displays in Texas. Residents compete — warmly and enthusiastically — for awards across dozens of decorating categories, and the city coordinates a driving tour that brings visitors from across San Antonio and beyond. It's an authentic community tradition, not a commercial production, and it says something meaningful about the character of the people who choose to live here. Neighbors who've been doing it for twenty years pass tips to newcomers. That's Windcrest.

Dining, Shopping, and Everyday Conveniences

Windcrest itself is residentially focused, but the surrounding 78239 and northeast San Antonio corridor offers solid everyday convenience. The Windcrest Town Center area along Windcrest Drive and Pat Booker Road has neighborhood-level retail. The Forum at Olympia Parkway and Randolph Marketplace in nearby Universal City are both easy drives for larger shopping needs. H-E-B — the beloved Texas grocery institution — has locations within comfortable reach, and the broader northeast corridor continues to develop with new retail and restaurant options.

Outdoor Living and Green Space

For those of us who believe that a home's outdoor space is just as important as its square footage indoors, Windcrest is particularly appealing. The lots are generous, the trees are old and beautiful, and the community park system is well-maintained. Gardeners will find the central Texas climate simultaneously challenging and rewarding — summer heat demands smart plant selection and irrigation planning, but the mild winters and long growing seasons mean something is almost always blooming. Live oaks, Texas mountain laurel, and lantana thrive here. Neighbors swap plants and garden advice more than you might expect.

Who Chooses to Live in Windcrest?

Over the years of working in the San Antonio market, I've noticed that Windcrest attracts a consistent type of buyer: someone who values authenticity over trendiness, community over anonymity, and stability over constant change. Military families appreciate the proximity to multiple installations and the neighborly culture that supports frequent PCS moves and transitions. Long-term San Antonio families choose it for its established feel and value relative to the northwest side. First-time buyers find it accessible without feeling like they're settling. And empty nesters frequently stay — or even downsize into the neighborhood — because they don't want to leave the community they've built here.

It's the kind of neighborhood where people introduce themselves when you move in. Where residents show up to city council meetings. Where the Christmas lights aren't just decoration — they're a statement about what kind of neighbor you intend to be.

Is Windcrest, TX Right for You?

Like any good garden, Windcrest rewards patience and attention. It's not a flashy new-construction neighborhood with granite countertops in every listing. It's a place that requires you to look a little deeper — to see the potential in a well-built 1960s brick home with 80-year-old live oaks in the backyard, to appreciate the quiet streets and the neighbors who wave from their driveways. If you're willing to look past surface-level curb appeal and invest in a home that has real roots, Windcrest will give you an extraordinary return — not just financially, but in quality of daily life.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the average home price in Windcrest, TX (78239) in 2026?

As of mid-2026, the median home price in Windcrest, Texas generally falls between $225,000 and $310,000, with significant variation based on square footage, condition, and lot size. Entry-level homes — typically 3-bedroom, 1–2 bath brick ranch styles in need of cosmetic updates — can be found in the $200,000–$250,000 range. Move-in-ready, updated homes with modern finishes typically list in the $250,000–$290,000 range. Larger or significantly renovated properties may exceed $300,000. Windcrest tends to offer better value per square foot than comparable neighborhoods on San Antonio's northwest side, while still delivering the stability and appreciation of an established, in-demand community. Because this is a small city with limited inventory, the market moves quickly when well-priced homes come available — working with a local buyer's agent who monitors this area closely is genuinely important.

What school district serves Windcrest, TX, and what schools would my children attend?

Windcrest falls within the Judson Independent School District (JISD), which serves much of the northeast San Antonio and Universal City area. Most Windcrest residents are zoned to Windcrest Elementary School (conveniently located within the neighborhood itself), Judson Middle School, and Wagner High School for grades 9–12. Judson ISD also offers magnet and choice programs that allow families to apply for specialized academic tracks — including STEM, fine arts, and career/technical pathways — at various campuses across the district. School attendance boundaries can and do change, so it's always worth verifying your specific address with Judson ISD directly before making a purchase decision based on school zoning. The district's website and enrollment office are responsive and a good first stop for current information.

How are property taxes in Windcrest, TX compared to the rest of San Antonio?

Property taxes in Windcrest are assessed under Bexar County jurisdiction, with the City of Windcrest levy added on top of the county, school district, and other applicable taxing entities. The total effective property tax rate in the 78239 area typically falls in the 2.1%–2.4% range, which is broadly comparable to much of northeast Bexar County and not dramatically different from other San Antonio-area neighborhoods. Texas offers meaningful exemptions that can significantly reduce your tax burden: the standard homestead exemption is available to all primary residence owners, and additional exemptions exist for homeowners over 65 (including a school district tax freeze), disabled individuals, and veterans with service-connected disabilities. Disabled veterans with a 100% rating may qualify for a complete property tax exemption in Texas — a significant financial benefit that draws many military families to the San Antonio market. Always calculate your expected tax burden using the actual assessed value and current rates for any specific property before closing.

Is Windcrest, TX a good place for military families?

Windcrest is genuinely one of the better neighborhoods in San Antonio for military families, and that's not marketing language — it's reflected in the community's demographics and culture. The city sits within 10–20 minutes of multiple major installations: Fort Sam Houston / JBSA-Fort Sam is approximately 10–15 minutes away, Randolph Air Force Base (Randolph Field) is accessible via IH-35 in about 15–20 minutes, and Lackland AFB is reachable in roughly 30–40 minutes via Loop 410. VA loans are extremely well-understood by local lenders and real estate professionals in this market, and the community has a long history of welcoming military families navigating PCS moves. The neighborly culture in Windcrest — where residents genuinely introduce themselves and look out for one another — also makes the difficult transition of a military relocation feel a little easier. Organizations like USAA, Veterans United, and local lenders such as Security Service Federal Credit Union have strong footing in the northeast San Antonio market and are experienced with VA financing.

What is there to do in Windcrest, TX, and what is the community like day-to-day?

Day-to-day life in Windcrest has a distinctly small-town quality despite being embedded within a major metro area. The city maintains its own parks system, including Windcrest City Park, which offers green space, playgrounds, and a gathering place for community events. The Windcrest Recreation Center provides programming for residents of all ages. The community's most famous tradition is its annual Christmas lights display each December — a decades-old competitive decorating event that turns the entire neighborhood into a holiday destination attracting visitors from across San Antonio and the surrounding region. It's a genuine community institution that reveals a lot about the character of the people who choose to live here. Beyond the neighborhood itself, residents have easy access to dining, shopping, and entertainment throughout the northeast San Antonio corridor, including Universal City, Selma, and the broader Loop 410 corridor. H-E-B grocery stores, the Forum at Olympia Parkway, and a growing selection of restaurants are all within a comfortable drive. For those who enjoy outdoor living, the generous lot sizes and mature tree canopy throughout Windcrest make gardening, outdoor entertaining, and simply enjoying a shaded backyard a genuine daily pleasure.
