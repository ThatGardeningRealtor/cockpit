Living in Schertz, TX: A Complete Guide for Home Buyers and Families

By Chris Drawdy · May 16, 2026 · 11 min read

Schertz is one of the fastest-growing cities in the San Antonio metro — and for good reason. From its tight-knit military community and top-rated schools to USDA-eligible pockets and easy access to JBSA, this suburb has become one of the most sought-after ZIP codes for families who want roots, not just a roof. In this guide, you'll learn exactly what makes Schertz tick: the neighborhoods, the commute realities, the financing advantages, and why so many people who move here never want to leave.

Why Schertz Has Become One of the Most Sought-After Suburbs in San Antonio

Schertz sits at the northeastern edge of the San Antonio metro, tucked between I-35 and I-10 in a way that makes it surprisingly well-connected. It's close enough to the city for work and play, but far enough out that you still get that small-town breathing room — the kind of place where neighbors wave from front porches and kids actually play outside.

What's driven the growth here isn't just affordability, though that's certainly part of it. It's the combination of excellent schools, military proximity, newer construction, and a city government that has consistently invested in parks, trails, and quality infrastructure. When a community puts care into its public spaces, it tends to attract people who care about community — and that's exactly the kind of neighbor most families want.

Think of Schertz like a garden that was planted with real intention. The soil was prepared well — good schools, strategic location, civic investment — and now the growth you're seeing isn't weeds. It's something that was tended carefully over time.

The Military Connection: JBSA and What It Means for Schertz Real Estate

Joint Base San Antonio-Randolph is approximately 10 minutes from the heart of Schertz, and that proximity shapes everything about the local housing market. A significant portion of Schertz homeowners are active-duty military, veterans, or DoD civilians — which means this community genuinely understands military life: the PCS moves, the tight timelines, the VA loan process.

VA Loan Advantages in Schertz

VA loans are extremely common in Schertz, and local lenders are well-practiced at closing them efficiently. Lenders like Security Service Federal Credit Union, Randolph-Brooks Federal Credit Union (RBFCU), and Caliber Home Loans all have strong VA loan teams familiar with Schertz-area transactions. VA buyers here benefit from no down payment requirements, no PMI, and competitive rates — and in this price range, that can translate to hundreds of dollars in monthly savings compared to conventional financing.

One practical note: because so many sellers in Schertz have gone through VA transactions themselves, VA offers tend to be received warmly here. That cultural familiarity is genuinely valuable when you're in a competitive multiple-offer situation.

BAH and Rent-vs-Buy Math

For service members stationed at JBSA-Randolph or JBSA-Fort Sam Houston, the Basic Allowance for Housing (BAH) for the San Antonio area in 2026 can cover a meaningful portion of a mortgage payment on a Schertz home. Many military families find that buying in Schertz — even on a short three-year assignment — pencils out better than renting, particularly in a market where home values have appreciated steadily. A good buyer's agent can help you run those numbers honestly before you commit.

Schertz Neighborhoods: Where Should You Actually Live?

Schertz isn't a one-size-fits-all city. Different neighborhoods offer different vibes, price points, and proximity to schools and amenities — so it's worth knowing what you're comparing when you start browsing listings.

Fairways at Scenic Hills

This established neighborhood near Schertz Pkwy offers mature trees, larger lots, and a more settled feel than newer developments. Homes here tend to be in the $280,000–$340,000 range and attract buyers who want character over cookie-cutter. The greenery in this area is genuinely lovely — the kind of neighborhood where you can tell people have been planting and tending their yards for decades.

The Crossvine

One of the most talked-about master-planned communities in the area, Crossvine sits along FM 1518 and features resort-style amenities, walking trails, a community pool, and thoughtfully designed home sites. Builders like David Weekley and Chesmar have active sections here. Prices typically run $340,000–$500,000+ depending on size and upgrades.

Homestead and Savannah Pointe

These neighborhoods offer newer construction in the $280,000–$360,000 range and are especially popular with military families because of their straightforward layouts, low-maintenance yards, and proximity to both Randolph and major retail along FM 3009. They're the kind of neighborhoods that are easy to move into quickly — which matters a lot during a PCS.

Schertz Parkway Corridor

Along and near Schertz Pkwy, you'll find a mix of older and newer homes, some with larger lots and more established landscaping. This corridor connects conveniently to IH-35 and is walkable to Schertz's recreational amenities including Pickrell Park and the Schertz Aquatic Center. For buyers who want more space to garden or let kids roam, this area often delivers more square footage per dollar.

Schertz Schools: What Families Actually Need to Know About SCUCISD

Schertz falls within the Schertz-Cibolo-Universal City Independent School District (SCUCISD), which consistently earns strong ratings from the Texas Education Agency. For families, this is often the deciding factor — and SCUCISD delivers across elementary, middle, and high school levels.

Samuel Clemens High School and Byron Steele High School both offer robust AP programs, dual-credit courses through Northeast Lakeview College, and strong UIL extracurricular traditions. Clemens in particular has a long-standing reputation for academic achievement and competitive athletics. Middle schoolers feed into Ray D. Corbett Junior High or Dobie Junior High depending on address.

Elementary schools like Wiederstein, Schertz, and Rose Garden consistently receive high marks from parents for attentive teachers and active parent communities. If you have kids starting school soon and want to map address-to-school assignments before making an offer, that's something I can help you think through — knowing which school feeds which campus can actually influence which street you want to buy on.

Commute Reality: Getting Around From Schertz

Let's be honest about commutes, because nobody benefits from sugarcoating them. Schertz's location along the I-35 corridor means you have real access to multiple employment centers — but I-35 between Schertz and downtown San Antonio does experience congestion during morning and evening rush hours.

A realistic commute to downtown San Antonio (Pearl District, Medical Center area, or South Loop employers) runs about 30–40 minutes during rush hour. Commutes to JBSA-Randolph are genuinely short — often under 15 minutes via FM 78 or Ware Seguin Road. JBSA-Fort Sam Houston is accessible in about 25–30 minutes via Loop 1604 and IH-35.

For those heading to the northeast — to Toyota's manufacturing plant on the south side or tech employers along Loop 1604 — Schertz actually sits in a favorable position that avoids the worst of downtown traffic entirely. If your job is in the Stone Oak or Westover Hills corridors, you're looking at a longer haul (45–55 minutes), and that's worth factoring into your decision honestly.

USDA Loans and First-Time Buyer Programs in the Schertz Area

This is one of the most underutilized advantages in this market, and it's worth your full attention. Certain areas on the outskirts of Schertz — particularly toward FM 1518 and into Cibolo — remain USDA Rural Development eligible, which means qualified buyers can purchase with zero down payment and no private mortgage insurance.

USDA eligibility boundaries shift periodically as population numbers change, so it's critical to verify current eligibility on the USDA's official map before assuming a specific address qualifies. Lenders like RBFCU, Gateway Mortgage, and Supreme Lending all actively originate USDA loans in this area and can run a quick check on any address you're considering.

Beyond USDA, Texas first-time buyers may also benefit from the Texas State Affordable Housing Corporation (TSAHC) programs, which offer down payment assistance of up to 5% and can be layered with FHA or conventional loans. The My First Texas Home program through the Texas Department of Housing and Community Affairs (TDHCA) is another option worth exploring — especially for buyers whose down payment savings are limited but whose income and credit are solid.

Parks, Outdoor Life, and Why Schertz Feels Like Home

For a community-minded person — or frankly anyone who values outdoor living — Schertz punches above its weight. The Schertz Aquatic Center is a genuine community hub, the youth sports leagues at Pickrell Park fill weekends with the good kind of noise, and the greenway trails winding through developments like Crossvine give residents a daily reason to get outside.

The Guadalupe River is a short drive away for tubing and fishing, and Canyon Lake is roughly 45 minutes out — close enough for a summer Saturday. Schertz has also invested in its farmer's market scene and community events calendar, including seasonal festivals that draw residents together rather than just passing through.

As someone who believes a home is really just the container for a life, and that the outdoors is where much of that life happens — I can say that Schertz gets this. The city has worked to give residents places to plant themselves, gather, and grow. That's not an accident; it reflects the priorities of the people who've built this community over decades.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Schertz TX a good place to live for military families?

Schertz is genuinely one of the best San Antonio-area communities for military families, and that's not marketing language — it reflects real structural advantages. The proximity to JBSA-Randolph (about 10 minutes) and reasonable distance to JBSA-Fort Sam Houston (~25 minutes) means minimal commute stress for service members. SCUCISD schools have long accommodated military families, meaning staff understand PCS timelines, mid-year enrollments, and the unique needs of kids who move frequently. Local lenders are VA-loan fluent, closing timelines here are well-practiced, and the community itself has a culture of welcoming new neighbors — partly because so many residents have been the new neighbor themselves at some point.

What is the average home price in Schertz TX in 2026?

As of mid-2026, the median home price in Schertz runs approximately $310,000–$360,000, though that range shifts meaningfully depending on neighborhood, age of home, and lot size. Newer construction in master-planned communities like Crossvine typically starts in the mid-$340,000s and climbs into the $500,000s for larger plans. Resale homes in more established neighborhoods — particularly along the Schertz Pkwy corridor or in Fairways at Scenic Hills — can offer more square footage in the $280,000–$320,000 range. Property taxes in Guadalupe County, where most of Schertz sits, run approximately 2.1–2.4% of assessed value, which is worth factoring into your monthly payment calculations.

Are there USDA eligible homes in Schertz Texas?

Yes — portions of the greater Schertz area, particularly toward the FM 1518 corridor and into adjacent Cibolo, have historically included USDA Rural Development eligible zones. However, USDA maps are updated as census data shifts, so the eligibility of any specific address in 2026 needs to be confirmed directly on the USDA's Property Eligibility site (eligibility.sc.egov.usda.gov) or through a lender who actively works USDA loans in this market. USDA loans require no down payment and no PMI, making them one of the most powerful financing tools available to moderate-income buyers. Income limits also apply — for a family of four in Guadalupe County, the 2026 limit is approximately $110,650, though this changes annually and your lender can confirm current figures.

How are the schools in Schertz TX rated?

SCUCISD (Schertz-Cibolo-Universal City ISD) consistently earns B and A ratings from the Texas Education Agency and has a reputation as one of the stronger mid-sized districts in the Greater San Antonio area. Both high schools — Samuel Clemens and Byron Steele — offer substantial AP and dual-credit college course options, which matters for families thinking ahead to college readiness and tuition savings. Extracurricular programs are robust, with strong UIL competition in academics, fine arts, and athletics. One practical thing families often don't realize: school boundaries within SCUCISD aren't always obvious from a map, so verifying the specific campus your child would attend based on a home's address — before making an offer — can prevent surprises and help you prioritize the right streets.

What new construction neighborhoods are available in Schertz TX?

Schertz continues to see active new construction in 2026, particularly in and around the Crossvine master-planned community off FM 1518, where builders including David Weekley, Chesmar, and Pulte have active phases. The area around Homestead and Savannah Pointe also has newer inventory in the $280,000–$360,000 range from builders like LGI Homes and Century Communities. One important note for new construction buyers: the on-site sales representative at any builder works for the builder, not for you — having your own buyer's agent costs you nothing (the builder pays that commission) and can help you negotiate upgrades, understand the contract, and avoid surprises during the build process. New construction timelines in Schertz currently run approximately 6–10 months for a spec build, though quick-move-in homes with shorter timelines are available in most active communities.
