Garden to Table

Fresh Tomato Bruschetta From the Backyard

When the tomatoes start coming in faster than you can eat them raw, bruschetta is the move. Dice them up with basil, a little red onion, garlic, olive oil, salt and pepper. Pile it on toasted bread. The garden does all the heavy lifting.

What made this batch different was the mix. Red heirlooms, yellow cherry tomatoes, different sweetness levels, different textures. The garden produces variety when you let it and that variety makes everything better.

This week's harvest. Red, yellow, big, small. The garden delivered.
 
What's Blooming

Passionflower Is Taking Over and We Are Letting It

Passionflower looks like it came from another planet. The blooms are intricate, alien-looking, and they open and close in the same day. It grows fast, climbs everything, and pollinators love it. Gulf Fritillary butterflies use it as a host plant so if you want butterflies in your yard, this is the plant to grow.

It's also native to Texas and incredibly drought tolerant once established. Perfect for a San Antonio garden that gets scorched every July.

Passionflower opening up this week. One of the most interesting blooms in the garden.
 
Summer Surprise

The Squash Is Already Setting Fruit

Squash blooms are one of the best things about a summer garden. That big yellow flower opens up, the pollinators do their thing, and a few days later you've got fruit forming at the base. This round zucchini variety is a good one for San Antonio, compact enough for a raised bed and productive all season.

Squash flower open, fruit already setting. The garden moves fast in July.
 
Fig Season

First Figs of the Season and Why Every SA Yard Should Have One

Fig trees thrive in San Antonio. They handle the heat, they don't need much water once established, and they produce more fruit than most families can eat. The Brown Turkey fig is the most reliable variety here, two crops a year, survives light freezes, and gets big enough to provide real shade.

First figs of the season came in this week. That first bite straight off the tree is one of the best things about growing your own food.

First fig of the season. Worth every minute of waiting.
