New Construction

The Builder's Rep Works for the Builder. Not You.

When you walk into a model home the person greeting you is friendly, knowledgeable, and genuinely helpful in many ways. But they are employed by the builder. Their job is to protect the builder's bottom line, not yours. That's not cynicism, it's just contract law.

Bringing your own buyer's agent costs you absolutely nothing on a new construction purchase. Builders budget the commission into their cost of sales, and that budget doesn't disappear if you show up unrepresented. It just stays with the builder. Having an experienced advocate in your corner at zero cost to you is one of the clearest wins in real estate.

One critical detail: most builders require your agent to be present or registered on your very first visit to receive commission and represent you throughout the transaction. Contact your agent before you ever drive to a model home. A five-minute phone call protects your representation for the entire build process.

San Antonio is one of the most active new construction markets in the country. Knowing the process before you arrive makes all the difference.
 
Builder Incentives

What Builder Incentives Are Actually Worth in San Antonio Right Now

Most major builders in San Antonio are offering incentive packages between $10,000 and $30,000 right now. These typically come as closing cost assistance, mortgage rate buydowns through the builder's preferred lender, or design center upgrade credits.

The rate buydown deserves special attention. A permanent 1-point buydown on a $400,000 home can save you over $260 per month and more than $93,000 over the life of the loan. But most buydowns only apply if you use the builder's preferred lender. Before you commit, get a competing quote from an independent lender like Frost Bank, Broadway Bank, or RBFCU. The math sometimes still favors the builder's lender after the buydown, but verify it yourself rather than assume.

Design center upgrades carry the highest markup in the entire transaction. A kitchen countertop upgrade that costs $10,000 through the builder might run $4,000 from a local contractor after closing. Take closing cost credits or rate buydowns when you can, and use local contractors for cosmetic upgrades on your terms.
 
Inspections

Yes, You Need an Inspection on a Brand New Home

This surprises a lot of first-time new construction buyers. A brand new home still needs an independent inspection. Municipal inspectors check for code compliance but they are not doing the same thorough inspection a licensed professional performs.

The stage before drywall is your best window to see everything inside the walls.

Common issues found in new San Antonio builds include improper HVAC duct sealing, grading problems that direct water toward the foundation, incomplete insulation, and minor plumbing irregularities that are cheap to fix before drywall but expensive after.

Pre-drywall is when a good inspector catches problems that would otherwise get buried in the walls. Don't skip this one.
