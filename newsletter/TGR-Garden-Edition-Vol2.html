Hey neighbors

Most people think gardening slows down when summer ends. In San Antonio it's the opposite. August and September are two of the best planting months of the year. This week we're talking about what to do right now to set your garden up for the strongest fall season possible.
 
Still Going Strong

The Front Yard Is Still Producing and the Harvest Keeps Coming

Peppers, tomatoes, okra, and more. The summer garden is still going and we're making the most of it before we start transitioning to fall. This week's harvest basket had banana peppers, jalapeños, heirlooms, and cherry tomatoes all at once. That's what a diversified garden looks like in July in San Antonio.

This week's harvest. Banana peppers, jalapeños, heirlooms, and cherry tomatoes all at once.
 
Fall Garden Prep

What to Do Right Now to Maximize Your Fall Season

The window for fall planting in San Antonio opens up in late July and runs through September. Here's what's worth doing right now:

Pull what's done. Spent summer plants that are no longer producing are taking up space and water that your fall crops need. Clear them out now.

Amend your soil. Add compost before you replant. Summer heat burns through organic matter fast and your soil needs to be replenished before fall crops go in.

Start seeds now. Tomatoes, peppers, and squash started from seed in late July give you a head start on a fall harvest. Broccoli, cabbage, kale, and collards can go in as transplants in August.

Plant strawberries. September is the best time to plant strawberries in San Antonio for a spring harvest. Get them in the ground before it cools down too much and they'll establish over the fall.

Strawberries are already in. Plant now and you'll have fruit by spring.
 
What's Blooming

Pride of Barbados Is the Star of the San Antonio Summer Garden

If you only plant one thing in a San Antonio summer garden, make it Pride of Barbados. It's native to South Texas, thrives in brutal heat, requires almost no water once established, and produces those incredible red and orange blooms from summer all the way through the first frost. Hummingbirds love it. Butterflies love it. And it looks incredible against a green background.

The Gulf Fritillary butterflies are all over the front yard right now too. If you planted passionflower earlier this season you're probably seeing them already. The lifecycle plays out right in front of you, eggs on the leaves, caterpillars, then butterflies feeding on the lantana.

Gulf Fritillary with wings fully open this week. Plant passionflower and they find you.

Even the Sunflowers Are Getting Ready for What Comes Next

The second wave of sunflowers is budding out right now. That's the thing about a garden that's been tended well. There's always something getting ready to open. Fall is just the next season doing what it's supposed to do.

Second wave of sunflowers budding out. Something new is always on the way.
