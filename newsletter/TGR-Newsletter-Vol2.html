Pricing Strategy

What the Showing Data Is Actually Telling You About Your Price.

Most sellers assume that if their home is getting showings, the price is in the right range. Not exactly. Here's the signal chart that most agents never talk about:

What you're seeing What it means
No showings in week one ~8% overpriced
Showings but no offers ~5% overpriced
Lots of showings, still no offers ~2% overpriced + something physical
Showings and offers right away Priced right

When you overprice a house you have to drop the price later. By then you've burned through your launch momentum, the algorithm has moved on, and buyers have noticed the reduction in the history. That's the last position you want to be in.

I used this exact framework this week. Looked at the data on an active listing in Laura Heights, had an honest conversation with my sellers, laid out all their options, and let them decide. We adjusted the price, rebuilt the photo sequence, and rewrote the entire listing description around feeling instead of features. The market responds when you give it what it needs.
 

Seller Tip of the Week

Your listing description should sell a feeling, not a feature list.

By the time a buyer is reading your description, they've already seen the square footage and the granite countertops on Zillow. What they're looking for now is permission to feel something about your home. Write to that. Every line should make them think this is the one.
