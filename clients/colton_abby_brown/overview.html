Your Path Forward

The renovation is the key. Every step forward on that work is a step toward a stronger outcome at closing. Here is the sequence that gets you there.

1

Determine what is left on the renovation

Kitchen, bathrooms, structural -- what is done and what is still in progress? This is the single biggest factor in your price and buyer pool. Finishing critical items (kitchen, baths, any mechanical) opens the door to conventional, VA, and FHA buyers. That is where the better prices live.

2

Request an updated payoff letter from your lender

The February figure has changed by now. You need the current exact payoff amount before signing a listing agreement -- this is the number that determines what you walk away with at closing. Call your lender at loanadministration.com and request a formal payoff statement.

3

Decide: finish first, or list as-is?

Finishing the renovation gives you the widest buyer pool and the best shot at a price that covers the payoff with something left over. Listing as-is limits you to cash buyers and likely means a gap conversation with your lender. Neither path is wrong -- but you should go in with clear eyes about what each one means for your numbers.

4

Sign the listing agreement and schedule professional photos

When the home is ready to show, I will have the listing agreement ready. Professional photos make a real difference for rural properties -- buyers cannot drive out to look without a reason, and great photos create that reason.

5

Go live, get under contract, close

Full MLS exposure across all platforms. I handle negotiations on your behalf. A well-priced home in this area that shows clean goes under contract in 30 to 45 days. Closing follows 30 to 45 days after that.
