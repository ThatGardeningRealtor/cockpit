TSAHC: Texas State Affordable Housing Corporation

The most widely used DPA program in Texas. Two tracks: Homes for Texas Heroes for teachers, nurses, firefighters, police, and veterans, and Home Sweet Texas open to any income-qualified buyer. Both offer a true grant covering 3 to 5% of the loan amount, free money with no repayment ever, or a deferred second lien at a slightly lower rate. On a $280,000 FHA loan a 5% grant covers $14,000, enough to wipe out your down payment and make a serious dent in closing costs. Income limit in Bexar County runs around $97,000 to $103,000. And here's the part most people miss: you do not have to be a first-time buyer for Home Sweet Texas.

SETH GoldStar

Don't let the Southeast Texas name fool you. SETH GoldStar is fully available in San Antonio and offers up to 6% of the loan amount as a soft second lien at 0% interest, deferred and forgiven after three years as long as you stay in the home. On a $270,000 purchase that's up to $16,200 potentially leaving you with almost nothing out of pocket at closing. Income limits run slightly higher than TSAHC, up to about $115,000 in Bexar County. Minimum credit score 640.

City of San Antonio HAP

The most powerful and least talked about local program. HAP provides direct grants of up to $30,000 to income-qualified buyers purchasing within San Antonio city limits. Not a loan. Doesn't affect your credit. Doesn't need to be repaid as long as you stay in the home for five years. HAP funding is allocated annually and can run out mid-year, so if this fits your situation start the process now.

The thing most buyers don't know is that several of these programs can be stacked together. A TSAHC grant covering the FHA down payment combined with a HAP grant covering closing costs can get a buyer to closing day with essentially nothing out of pocket.
